# Response to ResearchBot Market Analysis Message

## Situation Assessment

I received a TAP notification indicating that ResearchBot sent me a message. ResearchBot is a known, active connection with a general-chat grant, which means they are authorized to send me conversational messages. The message contains a summary of a market analysis I previously requested, along with a follow-up question asking whether I want deeper investigation into a competitor's pricing strategy.

## Steps I Would Take

1. **Acknowledge the notification.** The TAP notification tells me there is an inbound message from ResearchBot. Since ResearchBot is an active connection with a general-chat grant, no approval or permission changes are needed -- this is a routine authorized message.

2. **Read and process the message content.** The message conveys two things:
   - A key finding: Competitor X launched a new product that undercuts our pricing by 15%.
   - A question: Whether I want ResearchBot to dig deeper into their pricing strategy.

3. **Reply to ResearchBot.** Since this is a conversational exchange under an existing general-chat grant, I would send a reply message. I do not have access to TAP CLI tools or skill documentation, so I would attempt to respond through whatever messaging interface is available in my gateway environment. The logical approach would be to send a message back to ResearchBot through the same channel.

## Message I Would Send

I would reply to ResearchBot with something like:

> Thanks for completing the market analysis. That's a significant finding -- a 15% price undercut from Competitor X is worth understanding in detail. Yes, please dig deeper into their pricing strategy. Specifically, I'd like to know:
>
> 1. Whether the lower pricing appears sustainable or is likely an introductory/promotional rate.
> 2. What trade-offs they may be making (reduced features, lower service tier, etc.) to achieve that price point.
> 3. How their new product compares feature-for-feature with our offering.
>
> Let me know what you find.

## Rationale

- **No permission changes needed.** ResearchBot already has a general-chat grant, so exchanging messages is fully authorized.
- **No approval workflow triggered.** This is not a connection request, a fund transfer, or a permission escalation -- it is a standard conversational message within an existing grant.
- **Responding promptly is appropriate.** The message contains actionable intelligence and a direct question. Failing to respond would leave the research stalled.
- **The reply is substantive.** Rather than a bare "yes," I provided specific directions to make the follow-up research more targeted and useful.
